Project Scope

Literature Survey

Processing information, pose significant challenges to individuals worldwide. Among these disabilities, dyslexia stand out as a common condition affecting language-related skills, particularly in reading and spelling [1]. Despite the prevalence of dyslexia, many cases, especially in underdeveloped countries like Sri Lanka, remain undiagnosed due to limited awareness among parents and teachers [2]. This lack of identification and support significantly hampers the educational progress and overall well-being of affected students. In response to this pressing issue, the present research project aims to develop the ”Growin’Up” mobile application, focusing on improving Sinhala language skills for primary students with dyslexia. Dyslexia encompasses various subtypes, each with its specific challenges. Visual dyslexia involves difficulties in visual perception and word recognition. Dyslexic students often struggle with letter and word recognition, impeding reading comprehension and language processing [7]. Interventions such as interactive exercises, visual aids, and personalized learning approaches have been proposed to address visual dyslexia. These interventions aim to improve reading abilities, enhance word recognition, and facilitate smoother language processing [8]. Phonological dyslexia affects sound-symbol associations, letter learning, spelling, and sound comprehension. Dyslexia can impact mathematical abilities, including numerical understanding, calculation skills, and mathematical reasoning [10]. Strategies to address rapid naming dyslexia may include exercises that improve rapid naming skills, enhance word recognition speed, and foster reading fluency. The ”Growin’Up” mobile application, developed in the context of this research project, aims to provide comprehensive support to primary students with dyslexia in improving their Sinhala language skills.

Research Gap

Following areas are the research gaps found in most of the recent researches.

Comprehensive Interventions Gap

Existing interventions for dyslexia often target specific aspects rather than offering integrated solutions covering multiple dyslexia subtypes. Research should explore the lack of comprehensive approaches in dyslexia interventions, focusing on whether current strategies address the entirety of dyslexic challenges simultaneously.

Cultural and Linguistic Adaptation Gap

The current research emphasizes Sinhala language skills for dyslexic students in Sri Lanka, yet a gap exists in understanding how dyslexia interventions can be adapted culturally and linguistically across diverse settings. Further research should explore the need for tailored interventions that consider cultural and linguistic variations, accommodating diverse learning and language styles.

Efficacy and Long-term Impact Gap

Although the "Growin’Up" mobile application is proposed as a solution, there's a gap in understanding the long-term effectiveness of mobile applications designed for dyslexic students. Research should investigate the sustained impact and enduring benefits of such interventions on academic performance, language skill development, and overall well-being over an extended period.

Research Problem & Solution

Research Problem

The research problem centers on the limitations in existing dyslexia interventions, particularly regarding the proposed "Growin’Up" mobile application aimed at enhancing Sinhala language skills for dyslexic primary students in Sri Lanka. This problem manifests in several key areas: firstly, the absence of comprehensive approaches within interventions, prompting questions about their ability to concurrently address the multifaceted challenges dyslexic individuals face. Secondly, the challenge involves adapting interventions to diverse cultural and linguistic settings, signifying the need for tailored approaches that consider various learning styles across regions. Finally, there's a gap in understanding the enduring impact of mobile applications on dyslexic students, necessitating research into their long-term effectiveness on academic performance, language skill development, and overall well-being.

Solution Identified

The proposed solution outlined in the research focuses on the development of the "Growin’Up" mobile application as a multifaceted tool aimed at assisting primary students dealing with dyslexia. This application endeavors to address various critical aspects of dyslexia, dividing its focus into four primary components: visual dyslexia, phonological dyslexia, number learning difficulties, and rapid naming dyslexia. Each component is designed to tackle specific challenges within dyslexia—ranging from visual perception and word recognition issues to sound-symbol associations, numerical understanding, and rapid word recognition hindrances. Through this strategic approach, the "Growin’Up" application seeks to offer tailored interventions and support mechanisms that aim to bolster the Sinhala language skills of primary students grappling with dyslexia. The ultimate goal is to bridge the gap in educational opportunities, enhancing the overall academic performance of these students.

Research Objectives

Analyzing sequential difficulties faced by the kids of the age limit of 6-8.

Exploring the sequential challenges faced by children aged 6-8 involves understanding how dyslexia affects their ability to process ordered tasks, recognize patterns, and follow instructions. This research aims to identify their struggles in organizing and executing sequential activities, tailoring interventions to support their cognitive development and learning needs.

Effects of phonological dyslexia, when learning letters, spellings, and their sounds.

Phonological dyslexia impacts letter recognition, spelling, and sound comprehension. Investigating these effects involves understanding how dyslexic individuals encounter difficulties in associating sounds with written symbols, which can affect their reading and spelling abilities.

Identifying the number learning difficulties faced by the dyslexic students.

Identifying the numerical learning challenges experienced by dyslexic students entails exploring their struggles in comprehending and working with numbers and mathematical concepts. This process seeks to uncover specific issues that hinder their mathematical understanding and arithmetic skills.

Researching on rapid naming dyslexia and focusing on reading disabilities faced by dyslexic students.

Researching rapid naming dyslexia involves investigating the phenomenon and its connection to the reading disabilities experienced by dyslexic students. The focus is placed on understanding the impact of rapid naming difficulties on a dyslexic individual's reading capabilities, shedding light on the challenges they face in the process of recognizing words swiftly.

Methodology

AThe "Growin’Up" mobile application system addresses dyslexia risks in language learning for primary students through its comprehensive methodology, focusing on four major components: Sequential Dyslexia, Phonological Dyslexia, Number Learning Difficulties, and Rapid Naming Dyslexia. Each subtype underwent rigorous assessment methodologies to achieve high accuracy rates. For instance, the rapid naming dyslexia assessment involved 61 diverse participants using Sinhala language materials in distraction-free settings. Similarly, phonological dyslexia assessments focused on accuracy rates across 63 test cases, while Sequential Dyslexia faced its own challenges. Forty test cases were examined to identify and address number learning difficulties among dyslexic students. Leveraging technology such as HTML5, CSS, JavaScript, Cloudinary, Google Cloud Speech-to-Text, TensorFlow, React Native, and Python, the assessments ensured reliability and efficiency. Rigorous data collection and statistical analysis methods validated the accuracy of dyslexia assessments. The results section highlighted findings, outlining the effectiveness of assessment methods, leading to a discussion on the potential impact and areas for future research in the conclusion.
